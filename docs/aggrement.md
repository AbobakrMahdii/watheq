Contract for the Implementation of a University Graduation Project
Project: Document Verification and Forgery Detection System

**First: Contract Parties**

* **First Party:** Abobakr Siddiq Mohammed Mahdi – Lead Developer and Technical Supervisor of the project.
* **Second Party:** Ma’rouf Abdullah Mohammed Lah – Representative of the participating team in the project, authorized to sign this contract on behalf of the remaining team members.

The second party undertakes to distribute tasks among team members according to the agreed technical plan to ensure proper implementation and quality within the agreed deadlines.

**Second: Project Description**
The project involves creating an electronic system to verify and detect forgery in official documents. It includes:

* A web interface using **Next.js** For Admins
* A mobile application using **Flutter** For Clients
* A backend system (API and database) using **Django** For admins & clients
* A simple AI module using **Google Cloud Vision API** or light **ML algorithms**

**Third: Responsibilities and Duties**

1. **First Party (Abobakr):**

   * Develop the user interfaces for both the web and mobile apps.
   * Build the database system and APIs and integrate them with the interfaces.
   * Integrate basic AI functionalities into the system.
   * Provide technical and managerial supervision throughout all implementation phases.
   * Receive and integrate components developed by the team into the final system.

2. **Second Party (Team Representative):**

   * Prepare the required datasets and testing materials, including sample document images.
   * Participate in designing the initial wireframes and proposing ideas for user experience improvement.
   * Assist in initial testing, gather feedback, and deliver it to the first party.
   * Document the project workflow and prepare required academic reports.
   * Distribute tasks among team members and ensure delivery according to the agreed timeline.
   * Attend periodic coordination meetings (in-person or online via Google Meet/Zoom) with the first party.
   * Complete the remaining assigned tasks under the supervision of the first party.

**Fourth: Meetings**

* Weekly meetings will be held to review technical progress.
* Meetings may be conducted online or in person at a mutually agreed location.

**Fifth: Implementation Duration**
The project implementation period is **6–9 weeks** from the signing date of this contract, with the possibility of extension due to academic or technical circumstances upon mutual agreement.

**Sixth: Financial Compensation and Payment Method**
The agreed total amount for technical supervision and implementation is **$800 USD**, payable as follows:

1. **First payment (50%)** – upon contract signing and start of actual work.
2. **Second payment (50%)** – upon final delivery of the completed academic version.

Payment will be made in cash or via bank transfer, as agreed in advance.

**Seventh: Source Code Ownership**

* The first party commits to delivering the full project source code to the second party upon completion.
* After delivery, the team has full rights to use the code for **academic purposes only**.
* The first party’s name or any reference to them shall not be used outside the academic scope without prior written consent.

**Eighth: General Commitments**

* Both parties agree to maintain the confidentiality of project files and data during and after implementation.
* No external sharing or publishing of project content is allowed without mutual consent.
* Each party bears responsibility for delays caused by their own assigned duties.
* The first party retains the right to determine when the system is technically ready for final delivery.

**Ninth: Intellectual Property Rights**
The project’s intellectual ownership belongs collectively to the entire team for academic purposes. The project may be used for presentations or portfolios provided that team member names are not omitted.

**Tenth: General Terms**

* This contract is binding for both parties from the signing date.
* Any disputes shall be resolved amicably; otherwise, the issue will be referred to the university’s relevant legal department.
* Amendments may only be made by written agreement signed by both parties.
